Course Description

Studio Art builds upon foundational skills to develop advanced technical proficiency and personal artistic voice through exploration of historical movements, contemporary practices, and experimental techniques.

Essential Questions

Digital vs Traditional

How do digital tools expand and limit artistic expression compared to traditional media?

Authenticity & AI

What constitutes authentic artistic creation in the age of artificial intelligence and digital manipulation?

Cultural Sensitivity

How do artists maintain cultural sensitivity and avoid appropriation when working in global digital spaces?

Ethics & Responsibility

What ethical responsibilities do digital artists have regarding AI collaboration, copyright, and representation?

Digital Medium Impact

How does the digital medium affect the way art is created, shared, and experienced by audiences?

Human Creativity

What role should human creativity play as AI becomes more sophisticated in generating visual content?

Course Objectives

TECHNICAL SKILLS: Create professional digital design projects including package design, character development, and promotional materials with industry-standard quality
CREATIVE PROCESS: Synthesize research, experimentation, and reflection to develop sustained creative investigations that demonstrate conceptual depth
VISUAL LITERACY: Analyze and critique digital artworks using sophisticated understanding of both traditional art principles and digital-specific concepts
CULTURAL AWARENESS: Apply inclusive design principles that consider accessibility, cultural diversity, and equitable user experience across digital platforms
PERSONAL VOICE: Create digital portfolios that effectively communicate individual creative growth, professional competency, and unique artistic perspective
PROFESSIONAL PRACTICE: Apply ethical principles consistently in digital creative practice including proper attribution, fair use understanding, and cultural respect

COURSE UNITS â–ˆ

UNIT 1: Foundations and Digital Identity
How do artists establish their foundation and voice?
MAJOR PROJECT: Digital Traced Self-Portrait

Create a digital self-portrait that explores your artistic identity and establishes your digital workflow.

Step-by-Step Process

STEP 1: Wacom tablet introduction and interface navigation. Learn basic tools and pressure sensitivity settings.
STEP 2: Take reference self-portrait photo with proper lighting and composition.
STEP 3: Set up digital workspace and import reference image.
STEP 4: Begin digital tracing process, focusing on accurate proportions and basic shapes.
STEP 5: Apply digital rendering techniques including crosshatching, stippling, and blending.
STEP 6: Organize layers effectively for professional workflow.
STEP 7: Complete portrait with attention to detail and personal style.
STEP 8: Document your digital workflow process for portfolio.
STEP 9: Create a traditional comparison piece using same reference.
STEP 10: Write reflection on authenticity and digital vs traditional mark-making.

Essential Skills

Pressure sensitivity, digital rendering, layer management, digital workflow

Key Concepts

Digital drawing tools, tablet mastery, digital vs traditional techniques

Materials

Wacom tablets, digital drawing software, reference photography

Key Artists

David Hockney (digital painting), Petra Cortright, Casey Reas

UNIT 2: Digital Photography & Portfolio Setup
How do artists represent and interpret human experience?
MAJOR PROJECT: Themed Photography Series + Portfolio Launch

Develop a cohesive photography series and establish your professional web presence.

Step-by-Step Process

STEP 1: Learn photography composition fundamentals (rule of thirds, leading lines).
STEP 2: Develop theme for photography series based on personal interests.
STEP 3: Plan shooting schedule and location scouting.
STEP 4: Capture initial photos focusing on composition and lighting.
STEP 5: Set up Weebly website structure and navigation.
STEP 6: Continue photography series production (minimum 10 images).
STEP 7: Edit photos using professional software (Lightroom/Photoshop).
STEP 8: Integrate photos into website with proper sizing and formatting.
STEP 9: Optimize website for professional presentation.
STEP 10: Launch website and present series to class.

Essential Skills

Digital photography composition, lighting, photo editing, professional web presence

Key Concepts

Digital cameras, photo editing software, Weebly platform

Materials

Photography composition guides, lighting manipulation, digital photo editing, website navigation design

Key Artists

Gregory Crewdson, Cindy Sherman, Annie Leibovitz, Vivian Maier

STEP 7: Apply color theory to enhance visual impact.
STEP 8: Integrate character and typography for cohesive design.
STEP 9: Refine and complete poster design.
STEP 10: Present work and reflect on goal-setting integration.

Essential Skills

Digital color application, character development, poster design, typography

Key Concepts

RGB/CMYK color systems, character design principles, personal goal-setting

Materials

Digital design software, color management tools, design templates

Key Artists

Peter Halley, Takashi Murakami, KAWS, contemporary character designers

UNIT 4: Cultural Heritage & Design
How do artists preserve and celebrate cultural identity?
MAJOR PROJECT: Cultural Food Box Design

Design packaging for a cultural food item that celebrates heritage while demonstrating cultural sensitivity.

Step-by-Step Process

STEP 1: Learn package design principles and industry standards.
STEP 2: Research cultural food traditions and significance.
STEP 3: Analyze cultural symbolism and sensitivity considerations.
STEP 4: Develop design concept that honors cultural heritage.
STEP 5: Create package template with proper measurements.
STEP 6: Design graphics incorporating cultural symbols respectfully.
STEP 7: Apply typography that complements cultural aesthetic.
STEP 8: Create 3D mockup for professional presentation.
STEP 9: Present design to class with cultural context explanation.
STEP 10: Write reflection on cultural sensitivity in design.

Essential Skills

Template creation, typography, vector graphics, cultural research, 3D visualization

Key Concepts

Package design principles, cultural symbolism, graphic design, mockup creation

Materials

Digital design software, template tools, cultural research resources

Key Artists

Paula Scher, Stefan Sagmeister, cultural package designers

UNIT 6: Animation
How do artists push boundaries and explore new possibilities?
MAJOR PROJECT: Rotoscoping/Mixed Media Animation

Create an animation that combines traditional and digital techniques using rotoscoping.

Step-by-Step Process

STEP 1: Learn animation principles (timing, spacing, squash & stretch).
STEP 2: Introduction to rotoscoping technique and applications.
STEP 3: Record reference video footage for animation.
STEP 4: Plan frame sequence and timing studies.
STEP 5: Begin frame-by-frame animation production.
STEP 6: Continue animation with focus on smooth motion.
STEP 7: Integrate mixed media elements (traditional/digital).
STEP 8: Refine timing and add finishing touches.
STEP 9: Add sound considerations and final integration.
STEP 10: Present at animation festival and write reflection.

Essential Skills

Frame-by-frame animation, timing and spacing, mixed media combination

Key Concepts

Animation principles, rotoscoping techniques, traditional/digital integration
